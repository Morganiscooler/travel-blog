🇫🇷 The French Riviera 🇫🇷

My first stop in France was Monaco 🇲🇨 (It's just a coincidence the flag is Indonesia's aswell!)

Monaco is a tiny country with a small population of only 38.6k people, with 1/3 of the population being millionaires and the average salary ranging from $234,000 to $256,581, making it one of the wealthiest nations in the whole world!

Here are a few pictures I took of myself:

Pay attention to the background behind me, because ALL of those boats are private yachts owned by the wealthy citizens of Monaco! 🤑

The second most important reason to visit Monaco is to see the Prince's Palace of Monaco.

More specifically, at 11:55 AM everyday, the "Old Guard" hands over their duties to the "New Guard", with professionally coordinated movements and musical accompaniment.

Afterwards, I decided to eat a cheese quesadilla because a lot of the food in Monaco is super expensive (makes sense when the average person here is a millionaire..)

I also decided to explore town a little, the residents here definitely deserve the beautiful houses for such expensive prices!

The MOST important reason for Monaco's high tourism rate is because of the globally famous race team known as Formula 1 🏎️. While they didn't have a tournament the day we went to Monaco, it definetly is worth visiting if you're a big Formula 1 fan!

Here is a picture of Holden putting on a jellyfish hat at an aquarium gift shop before we left Monaco:

Half of our day was spent in Monaco, the other half was spent stuck in traffic. Check out these two Ferraris that also got stuck in traffic with us! 🔥

After the traffic, we began heading to Nice, and I followed the tour guide up the tower to take a photo of the beautiful ocean

I also decided to explore the town in Nice, here are some photos I took here:

I also decided to try some authentic French macaroons

My favorite flavor of authentic macaroons here in France is the rose petal flavor. It's definetly unique and stands out from the macaroons you would typically eat in an American Costco!

Before heading to dinner and leaving Nice, I encountered a local martial arts group that performed Brazillian capoeira and traditional music

Before witnessing this live performance, I had never heard or witnessed capoeira before. This was a total culture shock to me, and I am glad to now understand more about the local people in Nice and their meaningful ways of performing martial arts!

Here is what I had for dinner in Nice to finish off the day :)

The stew had amazing broth, and the pork was tender and juicy. The dessert was a little too sweet for me, so overall I would give this meal a 9/10.
