My second stop in France was Avignon, but we first went to the beach in Cannes.

There wasn't anything that special that happened during this time, but I did play a few games of beach football with Mr. Guyer and some friends in my group :)

After some beach fun in Cannes, we got on the tour bus to head to Avignon. While heading there, I saw the famous Mont Sainte-Victoire that Paul Cezanne painted in the south of France.

Next, the tour group arrived infront of the Papal Palace.

The Papal Palace of Avignon is an extremely important location for Western Christiandom. During the 14th century, popes from Rome would oftentime live lavishly in this fancy palace, where they would succumb to the king's control. As a result, this paved way for a bad reputation for the Catholic church, and later down led to the Great Western Schism.
