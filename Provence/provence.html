My last stop before leaving France was the province region.

During this time, we explored the ancient city of Nimes, which was a Gallic (Celtic) tribe that submitted to the Roman Empire back in 121 BCE.

Back in 1 BC, Nimes had been established as a Roman colony under the rule of Emperor Augustus, as this place was considered a regional Capital city (according to Britannica).

A common symbol and architectural pattern you will see when you go to Nimes is crocadiles chained to palm trees.

The famous symbol represents the city's ties within ancient Rome and its victory over Egypt in the Battle of Actium .

Our tour group then went into the ancient arena, where captured hostages used to battle eachother while the crowd cheers and watches.
