Barcelona was my second to last stop for the 2 week journey that I had been travelling through.

During this time, I learned about the unique architecture unique to Barcelona, as well as the unique language of this autonamous region known as Catalonia!

First, our group moved from Pont du Gard to a temporary 45 minute driver's break area. Here I tried a random restaurant's Pallela.

Next, our travel group arrived at a hotel near the calm beaches of Calella, Barcelona.

The hotel had a salt water pool, and I played a few swimming games with the travel group.

Here's some Spanish delacacies I tried in an outdoors restaurant in Puerta de Sol. (I unfortunately forgot to take pictures of my food during this time...)

The first image is a Calamari Sandwich, which is a sandwich made with white bread and deep fried squid rings. The second picture is a sweet, Spanish alcoholic cocktail beverage:

Okay, well that's enough of the food...

Time for some exclusive tourism spots in Madrid!

I also went to a modern art museum in Madrid. Here are some famous paintings I saw!

This first artwork isn't a painting, but a really pretty picture of graffiti I took :)

After my journey to the modern art museum, I headed to our assigned restaurant to eat dinner. The tomato soup appetizer was decent, not great.

The fried pork dish was not that good in my opinion, probably because it was deep fried in the worst way possible, and I was absolutely full from Horchata (rice juice) before dinner.

If there is a snack I could absolutely 1000% recommend while you're in Madrid, definetly try their authentic Horchata and Churros from Churreria Chocolateria!
